🌿 Plant care

Keep your plants thriving

Simple, honest care advice for every plant in our shop. Whether you're a beginner or a seasoned plant parent, we've got you covered.

💧Water when the top inch of soil is dry
☀️Most plants love bright indirect light
🌡️Keep away from cold drafts and radiators
🪣Always use pots with drainage holes

Monstera Deliciosa

Easy care

One of the most popular houseplants, the Monstera is forgiving and fast-growing. Perfect for beginners.

💧WaterWeekly
☀️LightIndirect
🌡️Temp18–27°C

Top tips

Water when the top 2 inches of soil are dry
Wipe leaves monthly to remove dust
Use a moss pole to encourage upward growth

Pothos

Easy care

Nearly indestructible, Pothos thrive in almost any condition and look stunning trailing from a shelf.

💧WaterEvery 2 wks
☀️LightLow–bright
🌡️Temp15–30°C

Top tips

Let soil dry out completely between waterings
Trim leggy vines to encourage bushier growth
Can be propagated easily in a glass of water

Snake Plant

Easy care

One of the hardiest houseplants available. The Snake Plant survives neglect and purifies the air.

💧WaterMonthly
☀️LightAny
🌡️Temp15–29°C

Top tips

Overwatering is the biggest risk — less is more
Thrives in low light but grows faster in bright spots
Repot every 2–3 years when roots outgrow the pot

Fiddle Leaf Fig

Advanced

A showstopper but a diva. The Fiddle Leaf Fig rewards patience and consistency with stunning foliage.

💧WaterWeekly
☀️LightBright
🌡️Temp16–24°C

Top tips

Never move it once it's settled in a spot
Water consistently — same amount each time
Mist leaves regularly to boost humidity

Rose

Moderate

Classic and rewarding. Roses need a little more attention but reward you with stunning blooms all season.

💧Water2–3x week
☀️LightFull sun
🌡️Temp15–26°C

Top tips

Water at the base — wet leaves cause disease
Deadhead spent blooms to encourage new flowers
Feed with rose fertiliser every 4 weeks in summer

Lavender

Easy care

Fragrant, beautiful, and drought-tolerant. Lavender is a low-maintenance superstar for pots or gardens.

💧WaterFortnightly
☀️LightFull sun
🌡️Temp10–25°C

Top tips

Plant in well-draining soil — it hates wet roots
Trim after flowering to keep it compact
Bring indoors during harsh winters

Orchid

Advanced

Exotic and elegant, orchids bloom for months when given the right care. Worth the extra effort.

💧WaterWeekly
☀️LightIndirect
🌡️Temp18–24°C

Top tips

Water by soaking the roots for 10 minutes then draining
Never let roots sit in standing water
After blooming, cut the spike to encourage re-blooming

Olive Tree

Easy care

Drought-tolerant and architectural, olive trees are perfect for sunny patios or bright indoor spaces.

💧WaterFortnightly
☀️LightFull sun
🌡️Temp7–30°C

Top tips

Needs at least 6 hours of direct sunlight daily
Use terracotta pots for good airflow to roots
Prune in spring to maintain shape

Bonsai Juniper

Advanced

A living art form. Bonsai require patience and skill but are one of the most rewarding plants to grow.

💧WaterDaily check
☀️LightFull sun
🌡️Temp5–25°C

Top tips

Check soil moisture daily — never let it fully dry out
Keep outdoors in summer, sheltered in winter
Prune and wire in spring to shape the tree

Basil

Easy care

Fresh, fragrant, and fast-growing. Basil is a kitchen staple that's incredibly easy to grow on a windowsill.

💧WaterEvery 2 days
☀️LightBright
🌡️Temp18–30°C

Top tips

Pinch off flower buds to keep leaves coming
Water at the base — soggy leaves cause rot
Harvest from the top to encourage bushier growth

Tomato Plant

Moderate

Nothing beats a homegrown tomato. With the right sun and feeding, you'll have a bumper harvest all summer.

💧WaterDaily
☀️LightFull sun
🌡️Temp18–27°C

Top tips

Feed with tomato fertiliser every week once flowering
Remove side shoots for stronger main stems
Support with a stake or cage as they grow

Mint

Easy care

Mint grows so well it can take over a garden bed! Keep it in a pot to control its spread.

💧WaterEvery 2 days
☀️LightPart shade
🌡️Temp13–21°C

Top tips

Always grow in a pot to prevent it spreading
Harvest regularly to keep it compact and bushy
Cut back hard in autumn for fresh spring growth

General plant care tips

💧

Watering

Most plants die from overwatering rather than underwatering. Always check the soil before watering — stick your finger an inch into the soil and only water if it feels dry.

☀️

Light

Bright indirect light suits most houseplants. Direct sun can scorch leaves, while too little light causes leggy, pale growth. A spot a metre from a south-facing window is usually ideal.

🪣

Drainage

Always use pots with drainage holes. Sitting in waterlogged soil causes root rot, which is one of the most common causes of plant death. Empty saucers after watering.

🌱

Feeding

Feed your plants with a liquid fertiliser every 2–4 weeks during spring and summer when they're actively growing. Hold off in autumn and winter when growth slows down.

🌡️

Temperature

Most houseplants prefer stable temperatures between 15–25°C. Keep them away from cold draughts, radiators, and air conditioning units which can cause stress and leaf drop.

🪴

Repotting

Repot when roots start growing out of drainage holes or the plant dries out very quickly after watering. Spring is the best time to repot into a pot one size larger.
